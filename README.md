# CS-255 Portfolio

## Artifact

**DriverPass Business Requirements Document**

This document describes the business requirements gathered from the DriverPass client. It includes the client's goals, functional and nonfunctional requirements, user needs and the overall purpose of the system.

**DriverPass System Design Document**

This document builds on the business requirements by presenting the system design. It includes UML diagrams and technical requirements that show how the DriverPass system could be designed to meet the client's needs.

## Reflection

#### Briefly summarize the DriverPass project. Who was the client? What type of system did they want you to design?

The client for this project was DriverPass, a company that wanted a better way to help students prepare for their DMV driving tests. The goal was to design a web-based system that allows customers to register for services, schedule driving lessons, take online practice tests, and access training materials. The system also helps employees manage appointments, customer information, reports, and user accounts.

#### What did you do particularly well?

I think I did a good job understanding the client's requirements and turning them into system requirements. I also created UML diagrams that helped show how the different parts of the system work together. During the project, I updated my design based on feedback so it better matched the client's needs.

#### If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?

If I had more time, I would improve the UML diagrams by adding more detail and making the layout easier to read. I would also spend more time reviewing the relationships between different parts of the system to make sure everything is as clear as possible.

#### How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your system design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?

I started by carefully reviewing the DriverPass interview to understand what the client wanted the system to do. Then I used those requirements to create the business requirements document and the system design. Considering the user's needs is important because the system should solve the client's problems and make daily tasks easier for both customers and employees.

#### How do you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a system?

I usually start by understanding the client's goals before thinking about the technical details. Then I organize the requirements and use UML diagrams to help plan the system. In future projects, I will continue using UML diagrams, ask for feedback early, and review the requirements throughout the design process so the final system better meets the client's needs.
